Gram Staining

Follow the steps precisely for accurate results.
Time left: 60s

Procedure Steps

  1. Prepare a bacterial smear on a clean glass slide, air dry, and heat fix gently.
  2. Flood the smear with Crystal Violet (Primary Stain) and let sit for 1 minute. Rinse carefully with water.
  3. Flood the smear with Gram's Iodine (Mordant) and let sit for 1 minute. Rinse carefully with water.
  4. Decolorize rapidly with 95% Ethanol/Acetone for 5–10 seconds until runoff is clear. Rinse immediately with water to stop over-decolorization.
  5. Counterstain with Safranin for 45 seconds to 1 minute. Rinse cleanly with water, blot dry with bibulous paper, and view under oil immersion lens.

⚠️ Common Mistake

Over-decolorizing with alcohol can wash the crystal violet out of Gram-positive cells, erroneously making them appear Gram-negative (pink) at the end of the procedure.

💡 Lecturer Will Ask

What is the function of Gram's Iodine? It acts as a mordant, forming an insoluble crystal violet-iodine complex inside the thick peptidoglycan cell walls of Gram-positive bacteria.
